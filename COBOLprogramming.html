COBOL Programming in Mainframes

It was a compiled English-like computer programming language designed for business use. It is an imperative, procedural and, since 2002, object-oriented language. COBOL is primarily used in business, finance, and administrative systems for companies and governments. COBOL is still widely used in applications deployed on mainframe computers, such as large-scale batch and transaction processing jobs. (Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COBOL) COBOL programmed mainframes cost $75,000.

COBOL Programming was named by a group in New York, and they called it COmmon Business-Oriented Language. It is a computer business language used in mainframes that can run across many different computer platforms. Mainframes have languages that can calculate for mathematics, but COBOL specifically calculates business equations like data processing. It is used daily because it has progressed over the years. COBOL went under development in November 1959. It was originally created in 1959 because of an idea that a programmer named Mary Hawes had. Her idea was taken to the Pentagon to begin development and gain funding. Magnetic tape was used to make COBOL to run, and it was used by many different groups of people. IBM started using COBOL in the early 1960s. It is now being used globally with major businesses relying on COBOL.
